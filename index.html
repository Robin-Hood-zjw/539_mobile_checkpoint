Personal Opinions

I am currently living in the States for my long-term graduate study and related research demands. Compared with my previous academic life in the UK, in I prefer the vibes in the States, and here are several reasons:

  • Compared with British universities, fewer interference conditions exist in the college environment of the States. Most of the US universities and colleges are built in the outskirts instead of downtown areas, so students and faculties have fewer chances to be influenced by surrounding entertainment, such as pubs, shopping malls, etc.

  • US schools' funding by far overweighs British schools'. What I feel shamed for British universities is that faculties from many British universities would go on strike within one month each year, and most of the students have few opportunities to receive different forms of financial aid from British universities. However, I can go to the free shop of my US uni and choose as many goods as I want, and I do not have to pay them, just because I am a student. Moreover, students are likely to receive academic promotions, such as graduate student instructors and research assistants. They can waiver some of the tuition fees and gain additional salaries.

Compared with the US, I feel like everything in the UK is more like porcelain, delicate but fragile, and not solid. The only reason I am grateful to my UK academic life is that it offers a platform and opportunities to help me go to the USA.
